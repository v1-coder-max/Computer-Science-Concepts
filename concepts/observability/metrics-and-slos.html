Metrics, SLIs & SLOs

Not all metrics matter equally. SLIs are the few that capture user happiness, SLOs are your targets for them, and the error budget turns reliability into a number you can spend.

#observability #metrics #slo #reliability

The idea in plain English

Start with what to measure. The four golden signals (Google SRE) are latency, traffic, errors, and saturation — measure these on every service and you'll catch most problems. (RED — Rate, Errors, Duration — and USE — Utilization, Saturation, Errors — are related shorthands.)

A Service Level Indicator (SLI) is a specific measure of the service's behavior — e.g. 'the proportion of requests served in under 200 ms' or 'the success rate.' It should reflect user experience, not internal trivia like CPU.

A Service Level Objective (SLO) is the target for an SLI over a window — e.g. '99.9% of requests succeed over 30 days.' A Service Level Agreement (SLA) is the contractual promise to customers (with penalties), usually looser than your internal SLO so you have headroom.

The clever part is the error budget: 99.9% allows 0.1% failures — that budget is something you can spend. Plenty of budget left? Ship faster, take risks. Budget exhausted? Freeze features and focus on reliability. It turns the eternal stability-vs-velocity argument into data, and it's why you don't chase 100% (impossibly expensive, and it leaves no room to ship).

Real-world example

🏢 How this works in production

SRE-influenced teams define a handful of SLOs per service (success rate, p99 latency), publish them on dashboards, and alert on budget burn rate rather than every blip. When a service blows its error budget, the policy is to stop shipping features and fix reliability until it recovers — a concrete, non-political mechanism. The four golden signals are the default first dashboard for any new service.

SLI → SLO → error budget

# An SLO expressed against an SLI (the success-rate indicator):
#   SLI  = successful_requests / total_requests
#   SLO  = 99.9% over a rolling 30 days
#   Error budget = 0.1%  →  ~43 minutes of downtime per month

# Alert on BURN RATE, not raw errors — page when the budget is being
# consumed fast enough to blow the 30-day window:
#   alert: error_budget_burn_rate > 14x  (fast burn → wake someone up)

# Golden signals to chart first: latency, traffic, errors, saturation.

Key takeaways

🎯 Remember these three

  • Measure the four golden signals (latency, traffic, errors, saturation); pick SLIs that reflect user experience.
  • SLI = the measure, SLO = the target, SLA = the customer contract (looser than the SLO).
  • The error budget (gap to 100%) turns reliability-vs-velocity into a number you spend; don't chase 100%.
